2. Delivery & Installation Labor

Material cost is only part of the story. Delivery and labor can add 40–60% to your total bill.

🚛 Delivery

  • • Dump truck delivery: $50–$150 per load (typically 10–20 tons per load)
  • • Distance surcharge: $5–$10 per mile beyond 20 miles from quarry
  • • Small quantity fee: Some suppliers charge $75+ minimum for orders under 5 tons
  • • Bulk bags: $3–$8 per bag if truck can't access your site

👷 Labor

  • • Spreading: $0.20–$0.50 per sq ft ($120–$300 for 600 sq ft)
  • • Compaction: $0.15–$0.40 per sq ft (plate compactor rental: $60–$100/day)
  • • DIY option: Save 100% of labor costs but budget 1–2 full days of work
  • • Contractor: Full-service install runs $2–$5 per sq ft all-inclusive

3. Hidden Extras (The Costs Nobody Tells You About)

These are the expenses that catch most homeowners off guard. Budget for them upfront and you'll avoid nasty surprises.

🏗️

Base Preparation & Excavation

If your ground is soft, muddy, or has old tarmac, you may need to excavate 4–6 inches and lay a compacted sub-base first. Cost: $0.50–$1.50 per sq ft ($300–$900 for a 600 sq ft driveway). This is the #1 hidden cost that people underestimate.

🧱

Edging / Borders

Without edging, gravel migrates onto your lawn and driveway edges erode. Steel or plastic edging costs $1–$4 per linear foot. For a 20×30 ft driveway (100 ft perimeter), that's $100–$400.

🌿

Weed Barrier / Landscape Fabric

Heavy-duty weed barrier fabric prevents weeds from growing through your driveway. Cost: $0.10–$0.30 per sq ft ($60–$180 for 600 sq ft). Skip this and you'll be pulling weeds within 6 months.

🔨

Compaction Equipment Rental

A plate compactor is essential for crushed stone driveways. Rental cost: $60–$100 per day. Most driveways require 1 day of compaction work. Without compaction, your driveway will develop ruts within weeks.

🚰

Drainage & Grading

If your driveway slopes toward the house or collects water, you'll need to grade the surface and possibly add a French drain. Cost: $200–$800+ depending on complexity. Poor drainage destroys gravel driveways faster than anything else.

5. How to Cut Costs by 20–30%

You don't have to spend top dollar. Here are proven ways to reduce your gravel driveway costs without compromising quality:

Money-Saving Tips

  • ✓ DIY the labor: Save $240–$600 by spreading and compacting yourself
  • ✓ Buy direct from quarry: Skip the middleman and save 15–25% vs. big-box stores
  • ✓ Order in bulk: Per-ton prices drop significantly for orders over 10 tons
  • ✓ Use crusher run for the base: Cheaper than washed gravel and compacts better
  • ✓ Share a delivery: Split a truckload with a neighbor and halve the delivery fee

Mistakes That Cost You More

  • ✗ Skipping the sub-base: Gravel sinks into soft soil — you'll redo it in 1 year
  • ✗ Ordering too little: A second delivery costs $75–$150 just for the truck
  • ✗ Using pea gravel for driveways: It rolls under tires — use crushed stone
  • ✗ No edging: Your gravel will spread across the lawn within months
  • ✗ Ignoring drainage: Water pooling = ruts and erosion within the first season
